🌸 Why May is Magical

You picked an incredible time to visit! May is widely considered one of the best months in Yosemite. Waterfalls like Yosemite Falls, Vernal Fall, and Bridalveil Fall are thundering at peak flow from snowmelt. Wildflowers bloom across the valley floor and meadows, and black oaks begin leafing out bright green against the granite walls.

Temperatures are mild and perfect for hiking — not yet the blazing summer heat. Crowds are noticeably lighter than June–August. Sunrise and sunset light in May is spectacular with clear air and long golden hours.

Note: Tioga Road and Glacier Point Road may still be closed mid-May due to snow. Always check nps.gov/yose for current road conditions.

🏠 Cabin Tips

Tent-cabins at Curry Village are cozy and social — walls are thin, bring earplugs.

Yosemite Valley Lodge — closest hotel to Yosemite Falls. More private rooms.

Bear boxes are mandatory for food storage — never leave food in your cabin or car.

Outside the park: El Portal and Mariposa have affordable options with less noise.

📜 Park History & Geology

Yosemite was carved by glaciers over millions of years, creating its signature sheer granite walls, U-shaped valley, and dramatic waterfalls. El Capitan is the largest exposed granite monolith on Earth. Half Dome's sheer northwest face was formed when glaciers peeled away half the original dome during the ice ages.

Yosemite became a national park in 1890, largely due to the advocacy of John Muir. The valley was inhabited by the Ahwahneechee people for thousands of years before European contact. Today it's a UNESCO World Heritage Site protecting 400+ vertebrate species and 1,400+ plant species.

    Essential Tips

    Things to know before you hit the trails

    ⏰

    Start Early

    Get to trailheads by 7–8 AM to beat crowds and secure parking. Popular spots like Happy Isles fill up fast.

    💧

    Stay Hydrated

    Bring at least 2–3 liters per person for longer hikes. Water is available at trailheads and some points along trails.

    🧥

    Layer Up

    May mornings can be 40s°F and afternoons reach 72°F. Pack a light jacket and rain layer — afternoon showers are common.

    🐻

    Bear Safety

    Never leave food in your car or cabin — use bear boxes. Keep distance from all wildlife and never feed them.

    🥾

    Wear Good Shoes

    Trails can be wet and slippery in May. Waterproof hiking boots with ankle support are strongly recommended.

    🌧️

    Mist Trail is MISTY

    Near Vernal and Nevada Falls you WILL get soaked in May. Bring a poncho — it's part of the experience!

    🚌

    Use the Free Shuttle

    Park once and use Yosemite's free shuttle system. 7 routes cover nearly all of Yosemite Valley.

    📵

    Download Offline Maps

    Cell service is spotty. Download AllTrails or Google Maps offline BEFORE entering the park in Groveland or Oakdale — both are on your route from Manteca.

    ☀️

    Wear Sunscreen

    At 4,000+ ft elevation you burn faster than at sea level. Apply SPF 30+ even on cloudy days.

    🎟️

    Half Dome Permit

    The cables require a permit. Check nps.gov for last-minute walk-up permits released 2 days ahead.

    🚗

    Parking Strategy

    Valley lots fill by 9 AM. Arrive early or take the El Capitan shuttle from the Valley Visitor Center.

    🍔

    Food Strategy

    Stock up on snacks and meals before entering — prices are higher inside. Degnan's is great for pizza after a long hike.
